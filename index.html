Classic Vanilla Cake

A light, fluffy vanilla cake with a tender crumb. Perfect for birthdays, celebrations, or just because!

Prep Time
15 minutes
Cook Time
30 minutes
Total Time
45 minutes
Servings
8 Slices

Ingredients

2 cups all-purpose flour

1¬Ω cups granulated sugar

¬Ω cup unsalted butter, softened

3 large eggs

1 cup whole milk

2 teaspoons vanilla extract

2¬Ω teaspoons baking powder

¬Ω teaspoon salt

Instructions

Preheat your oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans or line them with parchment paper.

In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes using an electric mixer.

Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the milk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix.

Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans.

Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean and the cake springs back when lightly pressed.

Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks to cool completely.

Once cooled, frost with your favorite buttercream or enjoy plain with a dusting of powdered sugar!

Butter Croissants

Flaky, buttery layers of French pastry perfection. A labor of love that yields incredible results!

Prep Time
2 hours
Cook Time
15 minutes
Total Time
3 hours + chilling
Servings
12 croissants

Ingredients

4 cups all-purpose flour

‚Öì cup granulated sugar

1 tablespoon active dry yeast

1¬º cups warm milk

1¬Ω teaspoons salt

1¬º cups cold unsalted butter (for laminating)

1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon water (for egg wash)

Instructions

In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Add warm milk and mix until a dough forms. Knead for 5 minutes until smooth.

Shape the dough into a rectangle, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 1 hour.

Pound the cold butter between two sheets of parchment paper into a 7x10 inch rectangle. Keep cold.

Roll out the chilled dough into a 10x16 inch rectangle. Place the butter in the center and fold the dough over to enclose it completely.

Roll the dough into a large rectangle, then fold into thirds like a letter. This is your first turn. Wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

Repeat the rolling and folding process two more times, chilling for 30 minutes between each turn.

After the final turn, roll the dough into a large rectangle about ¬º inch thick. Cut into triangles (about 4 inches at the base).

Make a small cut at the base of each triangle, then roll up from the base to the tip. Curve the ends to form a crescent shape.

Place on lined baking sheets, cover loosely, and let rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until puffy.

Preheat oven to 400¬∞F (200¬∞C). Brush croissants with egg wash and bake for 12-15 minutes until golden brown. Enjoy warm!

Soft Chapatis

Traditional flatbread that's soft, flaky, and perfect for scooping up your favorite dishes.

Prep Time
15 minutes
Cook Time
15 minutes
Total Time
30 minutes
Servings
10 chapatis

Ingredients

2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)

1 cup warm water (approximately)

1 teaspoon salt

2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee

Extra flour for dusting

Butter or ghee for serving (optional)

Instructions

In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well to distribute the salt evenly throughout the flour.

Add the oil or ghee to the flour mixture and rub it in with your fingertips until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.

Gradually add warm water, a little at a time, mixing with your hand. Knead the dough until it's soft and pliable. The dough should not be sticky or too dry.

Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes. This resting time makes the chapatis softer.

Divide the dough into 10 equal portions and roll each into a smooth ball.

Heat a flat griddle or tawa over medium-high heat. Dust your work surface with flour and roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 6-7 inches in diameter.

Place the rolled chapati onto the hot pan. When you see small bubbles forming (about 30 seconds), flip it over.

Cook the other side for another 30 seconds, then flip again. Gently press the edges with a clean cloth or spatula to help it puff up.

Once both sides have light brown spots and the chapati has puffed up, remove it from the heat. Optionally brush with butter or ghee.

Stack the cooked chapatis in a container lined with a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft and warm. Serve immediately with your favorite curry or vegetables!

Garlic Naan Bread

Soft, pillowy naan bread infused with garlic and butter. Perfect for dipping or as a side dish!

Prep Time
1 hour 30 mins
Cook Time
20 minutes
Total Time
2 hours
Servings
8 pieces

Ingredients

3 cups all-purpose flour

1 teaspoon sugar

1 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon active dry yeast

¬æ cup warm water

¬º cup plain yogurt

2 tablespoons vegetable oil

4 cloves garlic, minced

3 tablespoons butter, melted

Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Mix well.

Add warm water, yogurt, and oil. Mix until a soft dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.

Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.

While the dough rises, mix the melted butter with minced garlic and set aside.

Punch down the dough and divide into 8 equal portions. Roll each into a ball.

On a floured surface, roll each ball into an oval shape about ¬º inch thick.

Heat a large skillet or cast iron pan over medium-high heat. No oil needed!

Place one naan in the hot pan. Cook for 2-3 minutes until bubbles form and the bottom has brown spots.

Flip and cook the other side for 1-2 minutes until it puffs up and gets charred spots.

Remove from heat and immediately brush with the garlic butter mixture. Sprinkle with fresh cilantro. Keep warm under a towel while cooking the remaining naan. Serve hot!

Chocolate Fudge Cake

Intensely chocolatey and incredibly moist. This cake is a chocolate lover's ultimate dream!

20 minutes

10 slices

1¬æ cups all-purpose flour

¬Ω cup vegetable oil

Instructions

Preheat oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans or line with parchment paper.

In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.

In another bowl, beat eggs, then add coffee, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla. Mix until well combined.

Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. The batter will be thin - this is normal!

Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans.

Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs.

Heat a large skillet or cast iron pan over medium-high heat. No oil needed!

Let cakes cool in pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks to cool completely.

Once cooled, frost with chocolate buttercream or ganache. For extra indulgence, add chocolate shavings on top!

Slice and serve with a glass of cold milk or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the ultimate chocolate experience!
